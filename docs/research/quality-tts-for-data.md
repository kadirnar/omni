# Open high-naturalness TTS for assistant-side synthesis (state: 2026-07)

## Arena/eval context
Artificial Analysis Speech Arena (May 2026 snapshot) top open-weights: Fish Audio S2 Pro elo 1128.7 (Mar 2026, license unverified), Step-Audio-EditX 1104.9, Kokoro-82M 1056.2, Voxtral TTS 1055.9, Maya1 1050.6, Chatterbox 1006.4, Zonos 1000. Caveat: arena prompts are short neutral reads, which favors Kokoro's clean reading voice and penalizes expressive/dialogue models; for expressivity use EmergentTTS-Eval, paper MOS, and blind preference studies instead.

## Ranked candidates (naturalness/expressivity for dialogue)
1. **VibeVoice-7B** (mirror `aoi-ot/VibeVoice-Large`, MIT; official `microsoft/VibeVoice-1.5B` MIT; MSFT pulled 7B over misuse, MIT mirrors legal). Tech report MOS 3.76 vs Gemini 2.5 Pro TTS 3.66, ElevenLabs v3 3.40; WER 1.29, SIM 0.692. Dialogue-native: 90 min, up to 4 speakers, voice-prompt cloning. RTF ~0.5–0.8 (7B, H100/H200); 1.5B ~0.2–0.5. Failure modes: EN/ZH only, occasionally inserts spontaneous background music/sounds, no inline emotion tags.
2. **Higgs Audio v2** (`bosonai/higgs-audio-v2-generation-3B-base`, released 2025-07-22 as Apache-2.0; Llama-3.2-3B + 2.2B DualFFN audio adapter = 5.8B; 10M h pretraining; 25 Hz unified tokenizer). EmergentTTS-Eval win vs gpt-4o-mini-tts: 75.7% Emotions, 55.7% Questions; SeedTTS-Eval WER 2.44/SIM 67.7. Best open expressive prosody + multi-speaker dialogue. RISK: Boson pivoted to non-commercial Higgs TTS 2/3 (`license:other`), delisted v2 from org listing, and an Apr 2026 HF discussion disputes the tokenizer's Apache status — pin the July-2025 Apache snapshot/mirrors. Slow-ish (~3B AR, roughly RTF ~0.5–1 unbatched).
3. **CosyVoice3** (`FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512`, Apache-2.0, open-sourced 2025-12-15; paper arXiv:2505.17589; 1M h training, 9 langs; instruct-based emotion/style control; DiffRO post-training). Best WER-stability/robustness for bulk cloning; beats CosyVoice2 on content consistency, speaker similarity, prosody. `FunAudioLLM/CosyVoice2-0.5B` also Apache-2.0, RTF ~0.3.
4. **Step-Audio-EditX** (`stepfun-ai/Step-Audio-EditX`, 3B, Apache-2.0, Nov 2025). Highest-scoring commercially-usable open model on the arena (1104.9). Zero-shot TTS + iterative emotion/style/paralinguistic EDITING of existing audio — unique polish tool. EN/ZH-centric.
5. **Chatterbox / Chatterbox Turbo** (`ResembleAI/chatterbox`, `ResembleAI/chatterbox-turbo`, MIT, 0.5B Llama backbone, 500k h). Podonos blind test: 63.75% preferred over ElevenLabs. Emotion-exaggeration knob, 23-language v2, Turbo ~6x realtime. All outputs carry PerTh neural watermark (likely destroyed by Mimi 1.1 kbps retokenization, but verify). Arena elo below Kokoro on neutral reads; hallucinated words at low rate — needs ASR filtering.
6. **Index-TTS2** (`IndexTeam/IndexTTS-2`, Sep 2025, Bilibili). Best disentangled emotion control (reference audio, emotion vectors, or text descriptions via Qwen3) + duration control. License: non-commercial; commercial requires agreement (indexspeech@bilibili.com) — legal risk for training data.
7. **SoulX-Podcast-1.7B** (`Soul-AILab/SoulX-Podcast-1.7B`, Apache-2.0, Oct 2025, Qwen3-1.7B+flow matching). Multi-turn multi-speaker podcast dialogue >60 min stable, paralinguistic tags (<|laughter|>, <|sigh|>, <|breathing|>...), EN/ZH+dialects.
8. **Orpheus-3B** (`canopylabs/orpheus-3b-0.1-ft`, Apache-2.0, Mar 2025, 100k h). 8 emotive tags (<laugh>, <sigh>...) fire reliably; ~91 tok/s = realtime, vLLM 16–25 concurrent streams on H100. Zero-shot cloning mediocre; fixed voices good.
9. **Maya1** (`maya-research/maya1`, 3B, Apache-2.0, Nov 2025). Natural-language voice design + 20 emotion tags, vLLM realtime; arena ~Kokoro-level on neutral reads.
10. **Kyutai-TTS** (`kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr`, CC-BY-4.0, Jul 2025, actually 1.8B). Streaming, but cloning locked to precomputed embeddings (mimi_voice model withheld) — limits persona freedom.
11. Lower tier / excluded: **Dia-1.6B** (Apache-2.0, richest nonverbal tag set (laughs)(gasps)... but unstable voice identity without seed/prompt, EN-only, pacing drift → variety spice only); **MegaTTS3** (Apache-2.0, 0.45B DiT, but WaveVAE encoder withheld — cloning requires submitting WAVs to ByteDance queue → unusable at scale); **F5-TTS** (weights CC-BY-NC-4.0 via Emilia — non-commercial, no emotion control); **Zonos-v0.1** (Apache-2.0, artifacts/alignment issues, superseded); **Parler-TTS-large-v1** (Apache-2.0, 2024-era, below current bar); **Voxtral-4B-TTS-2603** (Mistral, Mar 2026, excellent but CC-BY-NC-4.0); **Higgs TTS 2/3** (2026, non-commercial).

## Real expressive corpora
- **Emilia-YODAS**: 114k h, **CC-BY-4.0** (commercial OK); original Emilia 101k h is CC-BY-NC-4.0; Emilia-Large (Feb 2025) = both, 200k+ h. `amphion/Emilia-Dataset`. In-the-wild spontaneous podcast speech, 6 languages.
- **Expresso** (Meta): 40 h (11 read/8 styles + 30 improvised dialogue/26 styles), 4 studio speakers, 48 kHz, CC-BY-NC-4.0.
- **DailyTalk**: 20 h, 2,541 two-speaker dialogues, CC-BY-SA-4.0 (`kyutai/DailyTalkContiguous` stereo version matches your dual-stream setup).
- **CANDOR**: 850 h, 1,656 dyadic video calls + transcripts; request-based academic data-use agreement.
- **Switchboard-1 R2 / Fisher** (LDC97S62; LDC2004S13/2005S13, ~260 h + ~1,970 h): 8 kHz telephone — too low-fi for voice quality; useful only for turn-taking/backchannel statistics; fees behind LDC login (historically low-$1000s/part, non-member).
- **YODAS2**: 500k h CC-BY-3.0 YouTube w/ transcripts (commercial OK, noisy). **People's Speech**: 30k h CC-BY-4.0 incl. spontaneous. **GigaSpeech2**: Thai/Indonesian/Vietnamese only — irrelevant for English assistant.

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt a three-layer recipe, all commercially-clean licenses:

1) Bulk dialogue synthesis (~2,000–2,500 h): VibeVoice-1.5B (MIT) for multi-turn two-speaker conversations — dialogue-native, long-form stable, strongest open subjective quality family — plus CosyVoice3-0.5B (Apache-2.0, instruct emotion control) for single-speaker assistant turns and voice diversity via zero-shot cloning of Emilia-YODAS (CC-BY-4.0) speaker prompts. SoulX-Podcast-1.7B (Apache-2.0) as a third generator for tag-driven nonverbals (laughter, sighs, breathing). At RTF 0.2–0.5, 2,500 h costs ~500–1,250 H100-hours: 3–7 days on your 8-GPU node.

2) Fixed-persona polish stage (~300–500 h): VibeVoice-7B mirror (MIT) with one curated voice prompt for maximum naturalness, then Step-Audio-EditX (Apache-2.0) iterative emotion/paralinguistic editing passes on that persona. ~400–800 GPU-hours. If a research-only track is acceptable, Higgs Audio v2 (pin July-2025 Apache snapshot) and Index-TTS2 are the top expressive alternatives, but both carry license risk in 2026.

3) Real speech mix for prosody diversity (~300–500 h): DailyTalk (20 h, CC-BY-SA) and a filtered conversational Emilia-YODAS subset (200–400 h, CC-BY) on both streams; Expresso (40 h, CC-BY-NC) only if you accept NC data; use CANDOR/Fisher solely for turn-taking/backchannel timing statistics, not audio.

Mandatory QC loop for all synthetic audio: Whisper-large-v3 WER filter (reject >5%), speaker-similarity gate, and an audio-event classifier to catch VibeVoice's spontaneous background-music insertions and AR hallucinations. Total budget ~1,000–2,000 GPU-hours — well within one node-week-scale run.
