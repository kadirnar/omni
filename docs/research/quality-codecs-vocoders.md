# What bounds acoustic quality in discrete-token speech generation

Three bounds, in order: (1) the codec's reconstruction ceiling (a frozen codec caps output quality at what its decoder can reconstruct from tokens); (2) how many of the codec's tokens your LM can model accurately (RVQ depth trades bitrate/quality against LM difficulty and latency); (3) the naturalness of training speech (separate workstream). Caveat on metrics: adversarially-trained decoders (Mimi, WavTokenizer) score poorly on PESQ/ViSQOL yet sound good — the Moshi paper explicitly shows adversarial-only training "majorly degrades objective metrics" while boosting MUSHRA to 81.0 vs 58.8 ([Moshi paper](https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2)); AudioCodecBench makes the same point ([AudioCodecBench](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.02349v1)).

## Mimi at 8/16/32 codebooks
- Mimi (kyutai/mimi, CC-BY-4.0, 24 kHz, 12.5 Hz, 2048-entry codebooks, codebook 0 = WavLM-distilled semantic) is trained with quantizer dropout over **32 RVQ levels**, so any prefix count works at inference ([kyutai codec explainer](https://kyutai.org/codec-explainer/)).
- Measured (Voxtral TTS, Mistral, arXiv 2603.25551, Mar/Apr 2026): **8 cb = 1.1 kbps, PESQ 2.07; 16 cb = 2.2 kbps, PESQ 2.67 (ESTOI 0.865); 32 cb = 4.4 kbps, PESQ 3.18, ESTOI 0.910**. Independent corroboration: NanoCodec paper measures Mimi@1.1kbps PESQ 2.13, SECS 0.678, CER 7.2 ([NanoCodec](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.05835v1)); AudioCodecBench gets 2.77. Kyutai's own explainer describes 8-level audio as "muffled… constant high-pitched noise," 32 levels clearly best.
- **transformers `MimiModel.encode(..., num_quantizers=N)` supports up to 32** (config default `num_quantizers=32`) ([HF Mimi docs](https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/en/model_doc/mimi)).
- LM cost of 32 cb: parallel-heads+delay is bad (31-frame stagger ≈ 2.5 s latency); a CSM-style depth transformer handles it with flat cost growth — **Sesame CSM-1B (Apache-2.0) and Kyutai TTS-1.6B (CC-BY-4.0, 1B backbone + 600M depth transformer) both generate all 32 Mimi codebooks** and sound natural ([sesame/csm-1b](https://huggingface.co/sesame/csm-1b), [kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr](https://huggingface.co/kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr)). CSM amortizes by training the depth decoder on 1/16 of frames.

## Other codecs (all speech-focused unless noted)
- **DAC** (descript, MIT): best raw fidelity — PESQ 3.69 @6 kbps, but 75–86 Hz frame rate, 9+ codebooks, no semantic stream → hostile to LMs ([AudioCodecBench](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.02349v1), [repo](https://github.com/descriptinc/descript-audio-codec)). 44.1 kHz @8 kbps and 24 kHz variants.
- **XCodec2** (HKUSTAudio/xcodec2): 50 Hz, single 65,536 codebook, 16 kHz, ~0.8 kbps; semantic-fused; powers Llasa ([HF](https://huggingface.co/HKUSTAudio/xcodec2)); a Jan-2026 update moves to 25 Hz/24 kHz ([arXiv 2601.20185](https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20185)).
- **WavTokenizer-large** (MIT): 0.48–0.9 kbps single codebook; worst fidelity in benchmarks (PESQ 1.88–2.17, SECS 0.576, CER 15) — reject.
- **BigCodec**: 80 Hz single codebook 1.04 kbps, good UTMOS but high frame rate. **SNAC** (hubertsiuzdak/snac_24khz, MIT): 0.98 kbps multi-scale — awkward grid. **stable-codec** (stabilityai, 25 Hz FSQ ~0.7 kbps): weights under Stability Community License; NanoCodec measured TAAE speaker-similarity collapse (SECS 0.323).
- 2025–26 low-frame-rate: **NVIDIA NanoCodec** (nvidia/nemo-nano-codec-22khz-1.78kbps-12.5fps etc.): at 1.1 kbps/12.5 Hz beats Mimi (PESQ 2.38 vs 2.13, SECS 0.788 vs 0.678) ([paper](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.05835v1)); U-Codec 5 Hz/32-RVQ PESQ 3.20 ([arXiv 2510.16718](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16718)). Interesting but swapping codecs breaks your frozen-Mimi stack.

## Renderers (tokens → waveform better than codec decoder)
- **CosyVoice2/3 flow-matching + HiFT** (FunAudioLLM, Apache-2.0, incl. Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512): chunk-aware causal CFM conditions on **their own 25 Hz FSQ (6561-entry) supervised semantic tokens + 256-d speaker embedding + reference mel** → mel → HiFT/HiFi-GAN 24 kHz; streaming-capable; full training code released ([CosyVoice2 paper](https://arxiv.org/html/2412.10117v1), [repo](https://github.com/FunAudioLLM/CosyVoice)). Not Mimi-compatible without retraining the CFM conditioning.
- **Chatterbox S3Gen** (resemble-ai, MIT): CosyVoice-derived flow-matching + HiFT over 25 Hz S3 tokens — proof this renderer family is adaptable/fine-tunable ([DeepWiki](https://deepwiki.com/resemble-ai/chatterbox)).
- **Qwen2.5-Omni Token2Wav** (Apache-2.0, inside Qwen/Qwen2.5-Omni-7B): sliding-window DiT flow-matching (2-block lookback/1 lookahead) → mel → modified BigVGAN; conditions on qwen-tts-tokenizer codes; Qwen has not released tokenizer training code ([tech report](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.20215)).
- **Kimi-Audio detokenizer** (MoonshotAI/Kimi-Audio, weights on HF): chunk-wise streaming flow-matching + BigVGAN from **12.5 Hz semantic tokens** with look-ahead — existence proof that 12.5 Hz semantic-only → natural waveform works ([tech report](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.18425v1)).
- **Vocos** (charactr/vocos-encodec-24khz & vocos-mel-24khz, MIT): ISTFT GAN vocoder, conditions on EnCodec tokens or mel — not Mimi. **BigVGAN-v2** (nvidia/bigvgan_v2_24khz_100band_256x, MIT, ~122M): mel→wav only; useful as final stage after any token→mel model.
- **No off-the-shelf open renderer conditions on Mimi tokens.** Voxtral TTS (mistralai/Voxtral-4B-TTS-2603, CC-BY-NC, Mar 2026) validates the hybrid at scale: AR LM emits 1 semantic token/frame, a small 3-layer flow-matching transformer generates the 36 acoustic-codebook embeddings per frame (8 NFEs), then the codec decoder renders — they argue FM beats a 36-step depth transformer on quality, compute and latency ([Voxtral TTS](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.25551v2)). T-Mimi (arXiv 2601.20094) re-architects Mimi's decoder for 9.6x speed at equal quality (no weights yet).

## Verdict: (a) vs (b)
**(a) Mimi@32 via CSM depth transformer + Mimi's own decoder** is the proven, low-effort jump: PESQ 2.07→3.18 ceiling, zero new components, validated by CSM-1B and Kyutai TTS. **(b)** has a higher ceiling (24 kHz mel + HiFT/BigVGAN) but requires training a flow-matching renderer on Mimi codebook-0 yourself (~100–200M model, single-node feasible; Chatterbox/CosyVoice code as scaffold), and semantic-only conditioning moves speaker/prosody control into reference embeddings.

## KEY FACTS
- Mimi reconstruction quality by codebook count (measured in Voxtral TTS paper, Mistral, 2026): 8 cb = 1.1 kbps PESQ 2.07; 16 cb = 2.2 kbps PESQ 2.67 / ESTOI 0.865; 32 cb = 4.4 kbps PESQ 3.18 / ESTOI 0.910 [https://arxiv.org/html/2603.25551v2]
- Mimi is trained with quantizer dropout over 32 RVQ levels, so any prefix (8/16/32) decodes validly; Kyutai describes 8-level audio as muffled with crackle and high-pitched noise, 16 noticeably better, 32 best [https://kyutai.org/codec-explainer/]
- HF transformers MimiConfig default num_quantizers=32 and MimiModel.encode(input_values, padding_mask, num_quantizers=N) accepts any N up to 32; decode takes (batch, num_quantizers, frames) codes [https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/en/model_doc/mimi]
- Independent Mimi@1.1kbps measurements: NanoCodec paper PESQ 2.13 / SECS 0.678 / CER 7.2 on MLS; AudioCodecBench PESQ 2.77 / STOI 0.88 on LibriTTS test-other — both confirm 8-codebook Mimi is the fidelity bottleneck [https://arxiv.org/html/2508.05835v1]
- Moshi paper: Mimi's adversarial-only training gives MUSHRA 81.0 vs 58.8 for EnCodec-style losses while degrading PESQ/ViSQOL — objective intrusive metrics underestimate GAN codec decoders [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2]
- Sesame CSM-1B (Apache-2.0) generates all 32 Mimi codebooks with a Llama backbone + small depth decoder, training the depth decoder on 1/16 of frames to amortize compute [https://huggingface.co/sesame/csm-1b]
- Kyutai TTS (kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr, CC-BY-4.0, 2025) generates 32 Mimi tokens per 12.5 Hz frame using a 1B backbone + 600M depth transformer, streaming, trained on 2.5M hours [https://huggingface.co/kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr]
- DAC (descriptinc/descript-audio-codec, MIT) has the best raw reconstruction (PESQ 3.69 @ 6 kbps LibriTTS) but runs at 75–86 Hz with 9+ codebooks and no semantic stream — poor fit for a 12.5 Hz frame-grid LM [https://arxiv.org/html/2509.02349v1]
- XCodec2 (HKUSTAudio/xcodec2): 50 Hz single 65,536-entry codebook at 16 kHz (~0.8 kbps), semantic-fused (frozen HuBERT features); a Jan-2026 revision moves to 25 Hz latent rate and 24 kHz sampling [https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20185]
- WavTokenizer (0.48–0.9 kbps single codebook) is the worst performer in 2025 benchmarks: PESQ 1.88, SECS 0.576, CER 15.3 (NanoCodec table); PESQ 2.17 in AudioCodecBench [https://arxiv.org/html/2508.05835v1]
- NVIDIA NanoCodec (nvidia/nemo-nano-codec-22khz-*-12.5fps, FSQ, 2025) beats Mimi at matched 1.1 kbps/12.5 Hz: PESQ 2.378 vs 2.132, SECS 0.788 vs 0.678, CER 3.6 vs 7.2 [https://huggingface.co/nvidia/nemo-nano-codec-22khz-1.78kbps-12.5fps]
- stable-codec (stabilityai/stable-codec-speech-16k, TAAE, 25 Hz FSQ): weights under Stability AI Community License (enterprise license needed above $1M revenue); NanoCodec measured severe speaker-similarity loss (SECS 0.323) at 0.7 kbps [https://huggingface.co/stabilityai/stable-codec-speech-16k]
- SNAC (hubertsiuzdak/snac_24khz, MIT) is 0.98 kbps with 3 multi-scale RVQ levels at ~12/23/47 Hz — hierarchical rates don't map onto a uniform 12.5 Hz frame grid [https://huggingface.co/hubertsiuzdak/snac_24khz]
- CosyVoice2 renderer (FunAudioLLM, Apache-2.0): chunk-aware causal flow-matching conditions on 25 Hz FSQ semantic tokens (8 channels x 3 levels = 6561 entries, ~0.317 kbps) + 256-d speaker embedding + reference mel, then HiFT/HiFi-GAN vocoder to 24 kHz; streaming and non-streaming in one model; full training code released [https://arxiv.org/html/2412.10117v1]
- CosyVoice3 is open: FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512 on HF, Apache-2.0, 9 languages, surpasses CosyVoice2 on content consistency/speaker similarity/prosody [https://huggingface.co/FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512]
- Chatterbox (resemble-ai, MIT) reuses the CosyVoice-style S3Gen renderer (flow-matching CausalMaskedDiffWithXvec + HiFTGenerator) over 25 Hz S3 tokens — evidence the CosyVoice renderer family can be retargeted to other token streams [https://deepwiki.com/resemble-ai/chatterbox]
- Qwen2.5-Omni Token2Wav (Apache-2.0, inside Qwen/Qwen2.5-Omni-7B): sliding-window DiT flow-matching (lookback 2 blocks, lookahead 1) generates mel chunks from qwen-tts-tokenizer codes, then modified BigVGAN; the tokenizer itself is not trainable/released standalone [https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.20215]
- Kimi-Audio detokenizer (MoonshotAI/Kimi-Audio, weights + code on GitHub/HF): chunk-wise streaming flow-matching + BigVGAN converts 12.5 Hz discrete semantic tokens to waveform with look-ahead for smooth chunk boundaries — existence proof for 12.5 Hz semantic-only rendering [https://arxiv.org/html/2504.18425v1]
- Voxtral TTS (mistralai/Voxtral-4B-TTS-2603, CC-BY-NC, Mar 2026): AR backbone emits 1 semantic token/frame; a 3-layer flow-matching transformer generates the 36 acoustic FSQ embeddings per frame in 8 NFEs with CFG 1.2; authors argue FM beats a 36-step depth transformer and MaskGIT on quality, compute and latency [https://arxiv.org/html/2603.25551v2]
- Vocos (charactr/vocos-encodec-24khz and vocos-mel-24khz, MIT) is an ISTFT-head GAN vocoder conditioned on EnCodec tokens or mel — fast but not Mimi-compatible and not a quality upgrade over Mimi's GAN decoder [https://huggingface.co/charactr/vocos-mel-24khz]
- BigVGAN-v2 (nvidia/bigvgan_v2_24khz_100band_256x, also 44khz_128band_512x, MIT, ~122M params) is mel-to-waveform only; standard final stage after token-to-mel flow matching (used by Qwen Token2Wav and Kimi-Audio) [https://huggingface.co/nvidia/bigvgan_v2_24khz_100band_256x]
- MusicGen-style delay stagger across 32 codebooks adds 31 frames = 2.48 s of intrinsic latency at 12.5 Hz, making the parallel-heads mode impractical at 32 cb; the depth-transformer mode costs 32 small sequential steps per 80 ms frame instead [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2]
- U-Codec (Oct 2025) reaches PESQ 3.20 / STOI 0.93 at 5 Hz with 32 RVQ layers, and NanoCodec/LFSC show 12.5–21.5 Hz FSQ codecs beating Mimi — but adopting any of them means abandoning the frozen-Mimi stack and retraining the LM's token space [https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16718]
- T-Mimi (arXiv 2601.20094, Jan 2026) replaces Mimi's decoder with a streaming transformer for 9.6x faster on-device decode at statistically equal quality (CMOS +2.3% within CI) — an efficiency drop-in, not a quality upgrade; weights not yet released [https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20094]

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt route (a) now: switch the frozen Mimi codec from 8 to 32 codebooks (`MimiModel.encode(num_quantizers=32)`, 4.4 kbps) and generate them with your already-implemented CSM-style depth transformer, decoding with Mimi's own decoder. This roughly moves the acoustic ceiling from PESQ ~2.1 ("muffled, crackly" per Kyutai themselves) to PESQ 3.18/ESTOI 0.910, and it is exactly what Sesame CSM-1B (Apache-2.0) and Kyutai TTS-1.6B (CC-BY-4.0) ship — both sound natural, so the path is de-risked. Do NOT use the delay-stagger mode at 32 codebooks (31-frame stagger = 2.5 s latency). Keep the backbone predicting codebook 0 (+ maybe 1-3) and let the depth transformer handle the rest; use CSM's 1/16-frame compute amortization to keep training cost near the 8-codebook run. This is days of config work, no new pretrained components.

Treat route (b) as the stretch upgrade, validated by Voxtral TTS (Mistral, 2026) and Kimi-Audio: LM emits only semantic tokens (Mimi codebook 0, 12.5 Hz) and a ~100-200M chunk-aware flow-matching model + HiFT/BigVGAN-v2 renders 24 kHz waveforms. No open renderer conditions on Mimi tokens today, so you must train one — CosyVoice2 (Apache-2.0) and Chatterbox S3Gen (MIT) provide full training code and architecture to retarget; single-node 8xGPU is sufficient. It has a higher naturalness ceiling but moves speaker identity into reference embeddings and adds weeks of training/integration. Sequence: ship (a), prototype (b) in parallel only if (a)'s naturalness still falls short.
