# Emotion perception → appropriate spoken response: survey (2024–2026)

## What production systems do

**GPT-4o AVM** (undisclosed mechanism): detects tone/affect and can render requested emotions, singing, whispering; OpenAI publishes no architecture. Independent measurement matters more: on ParaS2SBench (Nov 2025) GPT-4o Voice scores only **3.28/5** averaged over content+style appropriateness — even the flagship is largely "tone-deaf" to paralinguistics.

**Step-Audio 2** (Jul 2025): interleaved text + CosyVoice2 audio tokens; trains audio-grounded **chain-of-thought then RL (PPO length-limited reasoning → learned preference → GRPO 400 iters)** for paralinguistic reasoning; SFT scripts have LLM-generated **emotion descriptions and randomly inserted emotion/speed instructions**. Result: **83.1%** avg on StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic (11 tasks, 550 clips) vs GPT-4o Audio 43.5, Kimi-Audio 49.6, Qwen-Omni 44.2; emotion subtask 86 vs 82/66/76.

**Qwen3-Omni**: Thinker–Talker, 12.5 Hz multi-codebook MTP (same grid family as ours); expressiveness is **implicit** — Talker conditions on Thinker context; no explicit tags. **GLM-4-Voice**: 12.5 Hz single-codebook Whisper-VQ; emotion/dialect/rate control only via **user text instructions**, no user-emotion perception mechanism. **Moshi**: purely implicit; 20k h synthetic instruct speech, one actor, >70 styles; no perception supervision. **Kimi-Audio**: MELD SER 59.13; expressive via diverse-style conversation data. **EMOVA** (CVPR'25): semantic-acoustic disentangled tokenizer; the LLM **emits a discrete style label (24 = 2 speakers × 3 pitches × 4 emotions)** conditioning the detokenizer — a working response-style-tag existence proof.

## Academic mechanisms

- **(a) Discrete tags / structured think**: **ParalinGPT** serializes *current sentiment → response sentiment → response text*, improving both SER and response quality. **OSUM-EChat** (Aug 2025, Qwen2.5-3B + CosyVoice tokens) inserts a **⟨think⟩ stage with paralinguistic + semantic dual reasoning**; ablations are the cleanest evidence anywhere: removing the understanding-driven stage drops EChat-eval GPT-4o-judge scores emotion 58.0→53.4, age 63.1→35.4, sound-event 87.1→52.9; removing dual-think drops multi-label 72.0→56.4. **SD-Eval** (NeurIPS'24): conditioning responses on paralinguistic labels beats unconditioned counterparts in objective and subjective evals.
- **(b) Free-text captions/CoT**: **EmoOmni** (Feb 2026) 4-step emotional CoT (perceive → intent → strategy → respond; free-text emotion instructions passed to talker); w/o E-CoT response-coherence drops 1.81→1.36. **LPE** (Jan 2025) "listen–perceive–express" CoT works without paired emotional QA data. Cost: many extra text tokens — a problem for our 4–6 BPE-token/s text-ahead budget.
- **(c) Implicit only**: Moshi/Qwen-Omni-style. ParaS2SBench shows implicit models cluster near 3.0/5 and "produce similar responses even for contrasting queries" — implicit learning alone demonstrably fails.
- **(d) Auxiliary SER**: **BLSP-Emo** (EMNLP'24) builds empathy purely from ASR+SER data (emotion-aware continuations); extended by **OpenS2S**. **SpeechEmotionLlama** (Interspeech'25): behavioral alignment lets a *frozen* LLM respond empathetically without explicit SER labels — but that solves encoder alignment, not our from-scratch setting. **EACL 2026 industry (arXiv 2603.15981)**: joint RL on **3-class SER reward + LLM-judged tone-appropriateness reward** lifts Expresso response appropriateness **65→77%** (GPT-4o-Audio: 67.4%); authors explicitly rejected fine-grained emotion taxonomies as non-generalizable across datasets.

## Does explicit SER/understanding measurably help the S2S response?

Yes, three independent quantifications: OSUM-EChat ablation (above), EACL multi-task RL (+12 pts absolute), and ParaS2S: SFT with paralinguistic-labeled responses lifts Kimi-Audio 2.89→4.08, GRPO on judge rewards →4.44 (+11% over SFT, needing ~1/5 the annotations, 10 h demonstration warm-up). BLSP-Emo shows SER data alone (no emotional dialogues) already yields empathy. Nothing in the literature shows implicit-only matching explicit-perception systems.

## Benchmarks to track

**ParaS2SBench** (emotion 6-way, sarcasm, age, gender; 1,200 synthetic + IEMOCAP 709 + MELD 781 prompts, judge-scored content+style 1–5); **StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic** (HF: stepfun-ai/StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic); **SD-Eval** (7,303 utts, emotion/accent/age/environment); **EChat-eval**; **EmphAssess** (emphasis transfer, S2S prosody); **StyleBench** (2026, speaking-style control); AIR-Bench chat has speech-emotion subtasks. VoiceBench has no real affect track.

## Taxonomy learnability

Fine-grained SER is unreliable: Kimi-Audio, SOTA-tier, gets 59.13% on 7-class MELD; the EACL paper collapsed to 3-class sentiment for cross-corpus robustness; Step-Audio 2's 86% emotion is on a curated coarse set. Big-6+neutral (7–8 classes) is the practical ceiling for *perception*; response *style* can be richer (EMOVA 24 combos; Moshi 70 styles) because it's generation-side and self-consistent (synthesized with known labels). 16+ perceived classes: no system demonstrates reliable learning.

## Training mixture evidence

OSUM-EChat: paralinguistic labels ride the big understanding stage (50k h ASR+P), then only **~200 h / 200K empathetic conversations** in the final stage suffice; Step-Audio 2 sprinkles emotion instructions through general SFT scripts; ParaS2S achieves SFT-level results with 1/5 the emotional annotations via RL. Consensus shape: SER/paralinguistic supervision as a small persistent auxiliary task (~few % of tokens) through pretraining/SFT, plus a modest (hundreds of hours) emotion-labeled dialogue SFT set, optionally a judge-reward RL pass.

## Fit to our system

Our Mimi codebook-0 is WavLM-semantic-distilled (English-biased, prosody lives mostly in residual codebooks 1–31) — with 32 codebooks the acoustic evidence for emotion is present at input; what's missing is forced supervision to *read* it, which the auxiliary SER/tag task provides. Language-neutral special-token tags (unlike English free-text captions) transfer across languages for free and cost 2–3 frames of text-ahead budget instead of a full CoT paragraph. Data: Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512 (Apache-2.0) confirms instruct-controlled emotion/dialect/speed synthesis; SoulX-Podcast provides paralinguistic tags; ESD/IEMOCAP/MELD-style real SER corpora feed perception (check licenses; MELD research-only).

## KEY FACTS
- Step-Audio 2 scores 83.09% average on StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic (11 tasks, 550 clips) vs GPT-4o Audio 43.45, Kimi-Audio 49.64, Qwen-Omni 44.18; emotion subtask 86 vs 82/66/76; mechanism = audio CoT + RL (PPO then GRPO 400 iters) and emotion/speed instructions randomly inserted into SFT scripts [https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.16632]
- StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic dataset (gender, age, timbre, emotion, pitch, rhythm, speed, style, vocal activity) is on HF as stepfun-ai/StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic [https://huggingface.co/datasets/stepfun-ai/StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic]
- OSUM-EChat (Aug 2025) inserts paralinguistic+semantic dual reasoning inside ⟨think⟩ tokens; ablation: removing the understanding-driven strategy drops EChat-eval GPT-4o-judge scores emotion 58.0→53.4, age 63.1→35.4, sound-event 87.1→52.9; removing dual-think drops multi-label 72.0→56.4 [https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09600]
- OSUM-EChat needed only ~0.2K hours (EChat-200K, ~200K conversations, 107K with real-audio emotion labels) of empathetic dialogue in stage 3, on top of 50K h understanding (ASR+paralinguistic) and ~112K h generation data; base Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct + 4096-token CosyVoice codebook [https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09600]
- ParaS2SBench (ByteDance, Nov 2025): 300 prompts × 4 categories (emotion happy/surprised/sad/angry/fear/disgust; sarcasm; age; gender) + IEMOCAP 709 + MELD 781; existing S2S models are 'tone-deaf': GPT-4o Voice 3.28/5, Qwen2.5-Omni 3.25, Kimi-Audio 2.89, producing similar responses even for contrasting queries [https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08723]
- ParaS2SAlign: SFT with paralinguistic-labeled responses lifts Kimi-Audio 2.89→4.08; GRPO with judge-distilled reward reaches 4.44 (~11% relative over SFT) and matches SFT with 1/5 of annotations plus a 10 h demonstration warm-up [https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08723]
- Multi-task RL (EACL 2026 industry / arXiv 2603.15981): jointly rewarding 3-class SER correctness + LLM-judged tone appropriateness raises Expresso response appropriateness 65%→77% (GPT-4o-Audio: 67.4%); authors explicitly rejected fine-grained emotion taxonomies as non-generalizable across datasets [https://arxiv.org/html/2603.15981]
- ParalinGPT serializes current-sentiment → response-sentiment → response-text in one autoregressive stream, improving both sentiment prediction and response generation (Amazon, ICASSP 2024) [https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.15316]
- BLSP-Emo (EMNLP 2024) builds an empathetic speech-LLM using ONLY existing ASR + SER data via emotion-aware text continuations — no empathetic QA corpus needed; OpenS2S (2025) extends it to end-to-end S2S with an automated empathetic-dialogue synthesis pipeline [https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.03872]
- SpeechEmotionLlama (Interspeech 2025): training the speech encoder so a FROZEN LLM's response to expressive speech matches its response to style-conditioned text yields more empathetic responses — behavioral alignment without explicit SER labels [https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.01162]
- EmoOmni (Feb 2026) uses a 4-step emotional CoT (multimodal emotion perception → intention analysis → response strategy → response), with free-text emotion instructions to the talker; removing E-CoT drops response coherence 1.81→1.36 on ch-sims-v2 [https://arxiv.org/html/2602.21900]
- LPE (Jan 2025) 'Listen-Perceive-Express' shows CoT (transcribe → identify emotion → respond) enables empathetic spoken dialogue without paired emotional QA training data [https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.10937]
- SD-Eval (NeurIPS 2024; 7,303 utterances / 8.76 h across emotion, accent, age, background sound) demonstrates models conditioned on paralinguistic information outperform unconditioned counterparts in both objective and subjective evaluation; GPT-4o validated as judge [https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.13340]
- EMOVA (CVPR 2025) has the LLM emit a discrete style label (24 styles = 2 speakers × 3 pitches × 4 emotions) that conditions a semantic-acoustic-disentangled unit-to-speech detokenizer — a working discrete response-style-tag design [https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.18042]
- GLM-4-Voice (12.5 Hz single-codebook Whisper-VQ tokenizer, 175 bps) controls emotion/dialect/speed only via user text instructions; it has no explicit user-emotion perception mechanism [https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.02612]
- Qwen3-Omni Talker generates expressive speech by implicit conditioning on Thinker context (no emotion tags); audio tokens are 12.5 Hz multi-codebook with an MTP module for residual codebooks — architecture closest to ours among surveyed systems [https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.17765]
- Kimi-Audio reaches only 59.13 on 7-class MELD speech emotion recognition despite being SOTA-tier, showing fine-grained SER is unreliable; its expressiveness (top subjective emotion-control/empathy scores) comes from diverse-style conversation data [https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18425]
- Moshi is purely implicit: 20k+ hours of synthetic instruct speech from one actor covering >70 speaking styles/emotions, including instructions to speak with specific emotions; no user-emotion perception supervision [https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.00037]
- EmphAssess (EMNLP 2024) benchmarks emphasis preservation in S2S models with an automatic pipeline (EmphaClass F1 89% topline, En/Es) — usable for prosody-transfer evaluation [https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.14069]
- Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512 is Apache-2.0 on HF and supports instruct control of language, dialect, emotion, speed, volume — confirmed viable for synthesizing emotion-controlled multilingual assistant speech [https://huggingface.co/FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512]
- GPT-4o advanced voice: OpenAI demonstrates tone detection (raised voice as anger) and on-request expressive delivery/singing, but publishes no mechanism; its measured paralinguistic response appropriateness (3.28/5 on ParaS2SBench) is barely above other implicit systems [https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08723]
- StyleBench (2026) evaluates conversational speaking-style control of speech LMs — additional eval option alongside ParaS2SBench/SD-Eval [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.07599]

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt mechanism (a)+(d): discrete emotion special tokens in the inner monologue, backed by auxiliary paralinguistic-understanding tasks. Evidence: implicit-only systems are tone-deaf (~3/5 on ParaS2SBench incl. GPT-4o); explicit perception-before-response gives the largest measured gains (OSUM-EChat ablations; EACL-2026 multi-task RL 65→77%; ParaS2S SFT 2.89→4.08). Free-text captions (EmoOmni) work but burn our scarce 4–6 tok/s text-ahead budget and are language-biased; tags are language-neutral, aiding multilingual transfer.

Concretely: at each assistant turn start, the monologue begins with two special tokens — <user_emo:X> (perceived) then <style:Y> (chosen delivery) — before monologue text; the depth-transformer audio then realizes Y (EMOVA-style, but in-band). Add task-grid variants: SER-tagged ASR (asr + emotion token) and paralinguistic captioning, mirroring OSUM's understanding stage.

Taxonomy: 8 perceived classes (neutral, happy, sad, angry, surprised, fearful, disgusted, sarcastic/other) — 7-class MELD SOTA is only ~59%, and fine-grained taxonomies fail cross-corpus; keep 16 as tag vocabulary headroom but train/eval on 8. Response styles: ~12 tags + 3 intensity tokens (generation-side labels are self-consistent since we synthesize them). Total ≤24 of our 53 free special ids.

Mixture: paralinguistic-tagged ASR/captioning on ~3–5% of understanding tokens throughout; 200–500 h emotion-labeled empathetic S2S dialogues (~5–10% of dialogue SFT), synthesized with Fun-CosyVoice3 instruct (Apache-2.0) + SoulX-Podcast tags, with real SER audio (IEMOCAP/MELD — check licenses; ESD) for perception grounding. Optional later: small GRPO pass with a judge reward (ParaS2S: +11% with 1/5 annotations). Evaluate on ParaS2SBench, SD-Eval, StepEval-Audio-Paralinguistic.
