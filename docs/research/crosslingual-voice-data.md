# Data & Eval for Cross-Lingual Single-Voice Output (as of 2026-07-02)

## 1) Real same-speaker multilingual corpora (rare, but non-zero)
- **Common Voice polyglots — the best real source.** Common Voice tracks contributors by `client_id` across locales; ~4,907 client IDs appear in 2+ languages, and a 2026 audit found ~91% of them are genuinely the same speaker (~9% heterogeneous; filter with a verifier at cosine <0.4) (arXiv 2506.00733). **TidyVoice** (ICASSP 2026, arXiv 2601.16358, CC-BY-4.0) curates this: 212k monolingual + **~4,500 multilingual speakers over 81 languages**, with a **Tidy-X cross-language same-speaker trial set** — directly usable both as real training pairs and to calibrate cross-lingual speaker-sim thresholds. CV audio is CC0.
- **FLEURS** is n-way parallel *text* but different speakers per language — not same-speaker. **Multilingual TEDx / VoxPopuli / EuroSpeech** (78k h, 22 parliaments, arXiv 2510.00514): VoxPopuli interpreters are *different people* from the source speaker; a few MEPs/TEDx speakers do speak 2+ languages but there is no curated polyglot split — treat as opportunistic mining (speaker-verify across sessions), not a dependency. **CSS10** is single-speaker-per-language, 10 different speakers.
- Conclusion: real polyglot data ≈ tens of hours after filtering (CV clips are short, read speech). Useful for eval calibration and a small "real" fine-tune slice; **synthetic is the primary path**.

## 2) Synthetic cross-lingual pair generation (primary)
- **Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512** (HF `FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512`, released 2025-12, **Apache-2.0**): 9 languages — **zh, en, ja, ko, de, fr, ru, it, es** (+18 zh dialects). Covers **en/fr/de/zh, NOT tr**. Paper (arXiv 2505.17589) has an explicit **cross-lingual voice-cloning benchmark (CV3-Eval)**: reference in one language, text in another; SIM via **ERes2Net** cosine; en↔zh cross-lingual WER ~5.1%; zero-shot Seed-TTS-eval SIM (WavLM-large-ft): **0.780 test-zh / 0.718 test-en vs human 0.755 / 0.734** — i.e., at/above human same-speaker similarity. Streaming ~150 ms first packet; TensorRT-LLM 4x speedup.
- **Chatterbox Multilingual V3** (HF `ResembleAI/chatterbox`, **MIT**, 0.5B Llama backbone): 23 languages including **en, fr, de, zh, tr** — the only open model covering all five v1 languages. Card claims one 10 s reference "holds identity across every target language," but explicitly warns of **accent transfer**: "language transfer outputs may inherit the accent of the reference clip's language" unless reference matches the language tag. All outputs carry the **Perth neural watermark** (strip/flag consideration if used as training data — the watermark is in your training audio). Turbo variant (350M, en) RTF ≈ **0.5 on RTX 4090**.
- **VibeVoice** (Microsoft, Aug 2025): trained **en+zh only**; cross-lingual prompt→speech works and preserves identity/accent, but other languages are "experimental/unstable" per maintainers. Also note VibeVoice repo was pulled/re-released with usage restrictions — use for en/zh long-form only.
- **2026 newer options:** **dots.tts** (rednote-hilab, arXiv 2606.07080, June 2026, **Apache-2.0**, 2B, 1.5M h): SOTA open zero-shot cloning, **avg SIM 83.9 on MiniMax 24-language multilingual set** — strongest cross-lingual cloner with a clean license; check tr coverage on card. **X-Voice** (arXiv 2605.05611, May 2026, 0.4B, 30 languages, IPA-unified): its recipe is exactly ours — Stage-1 flow-matching synthesizes 10K h of speaker-consistent cross-lingual audio-prompt pairs, then fine-tunes with prompt text masked; promises open-sourcing. **Qwen3-TTS** now has an open GitHub org (verify weight license before use). Avoid F5-TTS/Fish-Speech/Llasa (CC-BY-NC).
- **Recipe:** ~300–500 seed voices (LibriSpeech/MLS/Emilia-YODAS, commercially clean, ≥10 s clean reference each) × 5 languages × ~40–60 utterances (~8–10 s each) → per-speaker ~30 min/language; construct pairs with **reference-language ≠ target-language** (20 directed pairs from 5 langs). ~1,000 h target: use CosyVoice3 for en/fr/de/zh (+instruct emotion tags), Chatterbox for all tr targets and as second-source diversity; cross-check with dots.tts. **QC filter every clip:** Whisper-large-v3 WER < 10–15%, LID = target language, SIM(gen, ref) gate below, DNSMOS ≥ 3.
- **Cost:** RTF ≈ 0.3–0.6 unbatched on 4090/A100-class (Chatterbox 0.5; CosyVoice3 with TRT-LLM ~0.2–0.3); batched serving gives ~5–10× real-time throughput per GPU. Synthesize 1.5–2× over target for ~30–50% QC rejection → 1,500–2,000 h raw ≈ **200–500 GPU-hours** (≈2–3 days on 8×4090; ~$400–1,500 at $1.5–3/GPU-h). ASR+SIM filtering adds ~50–100 GPU-hours.

## 3) Evaluation
- **Verifiers:** field standard is **WavLM-large fine-tuned for SV** (Seed-TTS-eval "SIM-o"; repo BytedanceSpeech/seed-tts-eval) and **ERes2Net** (CV3-Eval); ECAPA-TDNN(VoxCeleb) and WavLM-base-plus-sv are usable but **language mismatch causes known score shifts/degradation** (arXiv 2007.07689, 2110.09150, 2506.02777) — VoxCeleb-trained models are English-biased. Mitigation: use ≥2 verifiers, apply **language-dependent score normalization**, and **calibrate thresholds on TidyVoice Tidy-X** genuine cross-language same-speaker trials per language pair.
- **Thresholds in the literature:** SIM numbers are **not comparable across verifiers**. WavLM-large-ft scale: human same-speaker ≈ 0.73–0.76; SOTA TTS 0.70–0.78; a practical "same voice" gate is **SIM ≥ 0.60 same-language, ≥ 0.55 cross-lingual (expect 0.05–0.15 cross-lingual penalty), reject < 0.50**. VALL-E X (WavLM-TDNN scale) reported cross-lingual SIM 0.36 vs 0.30 baseline — old scale, don't mix.
- **Identity × emotion × language matrix:** for each seed voice, generate {5 langs} × {all emotion tags} × {neutral}; gates: (a) SIM(gen, ref) per cell ≥ threshold; (b) emotion-induced SIM drop vs neutral ≤ 0.05; (c) intra-speaker output–output pairwise SIM high/low-variance (identity stability); (d) LID correctness and per-language WER; (e) accent probe (native-listener or accent classifier ABX) since accent transfer is the dominant known failure.

## 4) Anti-spoofing/consent
Watermark all generated audio with **AudioSeal** (Meta, ICML 2024; code+weights **MIT** since 2024, localized sample-level detection) — 2026 practice treats cloned voices as biometric data requiring explicit consent + provenance marking; note AudioSeal is a flagging tool, not adversarially robust proof.

## KEY FACTS
- Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512 released 2025-12 under Apache-2.0 on HuggingFace, covering 9 languages: zh, en, ja, ko, de, fr, ru, it, es (+18 Chinese dialects) — includes en/fr/de/zh but NOT Turkish [https://huggingface.co/FunAudioLLM/Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512]
- CosyVoice 3 zero-shot Seed-TTS-eval SIM (WavLM-large-ft): 0.780 test-zh / 0.718 test-en for 0.5B vs human baseline 0.755 / 0.734 — at or above human same-speaker similarity [https://arxiv.org/html/2505.17589v1]
- CosyVoice 3's CV3-Eval benchmark has a dedicated cross-lingual voice-cloning subset (reference speaker in one language, text in another); speaker similarity computed as ERes2Net embedding cosine; en→zh cross-lingual WER ~5.1% [https://github.com/FunAudioLLM/CV3-Eval]
- Chatterbox Multilingual V3 (MIT, 0.5B Llama backbone) supports 23 languages including all five v1 targets en/fr/de/zh/tr; 10 s reference audio captures voice identity across every target language [https://huggingface.co/ResembleAI/chatterbox]
- Chatterbox card documents accent transfer: 'ensure the reference clip matches the specified language tag, otherwise language transfer outputs may inherit the accent of the reference clip's language' [https://huggingface.co/ResembleAI/chatterbox]
- Every Chatterbox output is watermarked with Resemble's Perth neural watermarker (~100% detection surviving MP3/edits) — synthetic training data from it will carry watermarks [https://www.resemble.ai/introducing-chatterbox-multilingual-open-source-tts-for-23-languages/]
- Chatterbox Turbo (350M) streaming achieves RTF 0.499 and ~472 ms first-chunk latency on an RTX 4090 [https://github.com/davidbrowne17/chatterbox-streaming]
- VibeVoice-1.5B is trained on English and Chinese only; cross-lingual narration with one voice identity works but other languages are experimental/unstable per Microsoft's repo discussions [https://huggingface.co/microsoft/VibeVoice-1.5B/discussions/33]
- dots.tts (rednote-hilab, June 2026, 2B params, 1.5M h training data) released code and all checkpoints under Apache-2.0; leads open-source average speaker similarity (83.9) on the 24-language MiniMax multilingual test set [https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07080]
- X-Voice (May 2026, 0.4B, 30 languages via IPA) validates the exact synthetic recipe: Stage-1 flow-matching synthesizes 10K h of speaker-consistent audio-prompt pairs, Stage-2 fine-tunes with prompt text masked for zero-shot cross-lingual cloning; authors state all resources will be open-sourced [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05611]
- Common Voice has ~4,907 multilingual client_ids (same contributor recording in 2+ language locales); ~9% (433) are heterogeneous (actually different speakers), so ~91% are genuine polyglots — filterable with speaker-verifier cosine <0.4 [https://arxiv.org/html/2506.00733v1]
- TidyVoice (ICASSP 2026, CC-BY-4.0) curates Common Voice into a multilingual speaker-verification dataset: 212k monolingual + ~4,500 multilingual speakers across 81 languages, including the Tidy-X condition with same- and cross-language same-speaker trials [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16358]
- VoxPopuli's 17.3K h of aligned interpretations are spoken by interpreters, i.e., different speakers from the source — not same-speaker multilingual data [https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.00390]
- EuroSpeech (Oct 2025) provides ~78k h across 22 national parliaments / 22 languages on HuggingFace (disco-eth/EuroSpeech), mostly open licenses, but has no curated polyglot-speaker split [https://arxiv.org/html/2510.00514v1]
- Seed-TTS-eval protocol: SIM = cosine similarity of embeddings from WavLM-large fine-tuned on speaker verification; WER via Whisper-large-v3 (en) / Paraformer (zh); this 'SIM-o' is the de-facto standard scale in 2025-2026 TTS papers [https://github.com/BytedanceSpeech/seed-tts-eval]
- SIM scores are verifier-dependent: VALL-E X reported cross-lingual (zh prompt → en speech) SIM 0.36 vs 0.30 baseline on the older WavLM-TDNN scale — not comparable with WavLM-large-ft numbers around 0.7 [https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.03926]
- Cross-lingual speaker verification suffers documented language-mismatch degradation and score shift; mitigations are language-dependent score normalization and cross-lingual fine-tuning (VoxSRC-20 winner recipe; follow-ups 2021-2025) [https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.07689]
- CosyVoice repo supports streaming inference with KV-cache/SDPA (~150 ms latency) and TensorRT-LLM giving ~4x speedup, implying unbatched RTF well below 1 and batched throughput of ~5-10x real-time per GPU [https://github.com/FunAudioLLM/CosyVoice]
- AudioSeal (Meta) is MIT-licensed including model weights (relicensed 2024), does localized sample-level watermark detection at 24/44.1/48 kHz, and is the standard open watermarker for generated speech; treated as a flagging tool, not adversarially robust proof [https://github.com/facebookresearch/audioseal]
- CSS10 is ten single-speaker corpora in ten different languages (one distinct speaker per language) — not usable as same-speaker multilingual data [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2207.01507]
- Amazon's polyglot-TTS study concludes same-speaker multilingual corpora are 'rarely practical to procure and unscalable', motivating synthetic/voice-conversion pair construction as the standard approach [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2207.01507]
- FLEURS is n-way parallel in text but uses different speakers per language, so it provides no same-speaker cross-language pairs (only parallel content for eval prompts) [https://github.com/FunAudioLLM/CV3-Eval]

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt a synthetic-first recipe with real-data calibration. Data: ~300–500 commercially-clean seed voices (LibriSpeech/MLS/Emilia-YODAS, ≥10 s clean reference each) × 5 languages × ~40–60 utterances, pairs constructed only where reference-language ≠ target-language (20 directed pairs). Generators: Fun-CosyVoice3-0.5B-2512 (Apache-2.0) for en/fr/de/zh including instruct emotion tags; Chatterbox Multilingual V3 (MIT) for all Turkish targets and as a second source for diversity; evaluate dots.tts (Apache-2.0, best open multilingual SIM) as a third/upgrade source; skip VibeVoice outside en/zh. QC every clip: Whisper WER <10–15%, LID = target, DNSMOS ≥3, SIM gate. Budget: synthesize ~1,500–2,000 h raw for 1,000 h kept ≈ 200–500 GPU-hours (~2–3 days on 8×4090, <$1.5k) — cheap; do it. Mine Common Voice polyglots via TidyVoice for a small real fine-tune slice and, critically, use Tidy-X cross-language same-speaker trials to calibrate per-language-pair thresholds. Eval gates: dual verifiers (WavLM-large-ft SV on the Seed-TTS-eval scale + ERes2Net per CV3-Eval); pass = SIM ≥0.60 same-language, ≥0.55 cross-lingual (calibrated against human baselines 0.73–0.76 and Tidy-X distributions), emotion-tag SIM drop ≤0.05 vs neutral, plus LID/WER and an accent-transfer probe (the dominant known failure mode — mitigate by mixing reference languages per seed voice). Ship AudioSeal (MIT) watermarking on all cloned output and require explicit consent for reference audio.
