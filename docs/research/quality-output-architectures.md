# How the most natural speech-LLMs (mid-2025→2026) make audio

Two dominant recipes exist: (A) **LM predicts full acoustic RVQ codec tokens, codec decoder renders** (Moshi, CSM, LFM2-Audio, MiMo-Audio, Qwen3-Omni); (B) **LM predicts low-rate single-codebook "semantic" tokens, a pretrained flow-matching token2wav renders** (GLM-4-Voice, Kimi-Audio, Step-Audio 2, Qwen2.5-Omni). The field's naturalness leaders use (B) at small-to-mid data scale; (A) wins latency and needs far more data/scale.

## Systems
- **Moshi (baseline, Kyutai, 2024)**: Helium 7B temporal transformer + small depth transformer over Mimi (12.5 Hz, 8 codebooks used, 1.1 kbps); 7M h audio pretrain + 20k h synthetic dialogue; 160 ms theoretical / 200 ms practical latency; full-duplex; intelligible but famously flat/robotic voice — proof that 1.1 kbps Mimi + limited fine-tuning caps naturalness.
- **Sesame CSM (Feb 2025)**: Llama backbone + tiny "audio decoder" (depth transformer): 1B+100M (released csm-1b, Apache-2.0), 3B+250M, 8B+300M. Uses Mimi with **32 codebooks** (1 semantic + 31 acoustic ≈ 4.4 kbps) — the released kyutai/mimi checkpoint already has num_quantizers=32 (Transformers MimiConfig default 32; encode/decode take num_quantizers). **Compute amortization**: zeroth codebook trained every frame, depth decoder on a random **1/16 of frames**, no measurable quality loss. Trained ~**1M hours** English, 5 epochs, 2048-token (~2 min) context. Naturalness: CMOS on Expresso showed **no listener preference vs real human speech without context** (humans still win with conversational context); virality came from context-conditioned prosody + turn timing (The Verge, Reddit blind reactions, Mar 2025 blind tests where users initially couldn't tell). Note: csm-1b is TTS-with-context, not an S2S dialogue LM.
- **Qwen2.5-Omni (Mar 2025, Apache-2.0)**: Thinker–Talker. Talker emits single-codebook codec tokens; **token2wav = sliding-window-DiT flow-matching (2-block lookback/1 lookahead) → mel → modified BigVGAN**. Streaming; renderer is why its voice is clean.
- **Qwen3-Omni (Sep 2025, Apache-2.0, 30B-A3B)**: Talker is a **3B-A0.3B MoE** predicting multi-codebook codec via **MTP module** (residual codebooks per frame); replaces DiT with a **lightweight causal ConvNet Code2Wav** for frame-by-frame synthesis; 12.5 Hz codes; **234 ms** end-to-end first-packet (audio). This is the industrial-scale version of your architecture.
- **Qwen3.5-Omni (Apr 2026, API-only, not open)**: same Talker+MTP+causal ConvNet; adds ARIA text-speech rate alignment; 235–435 ms first packet; SEED-TTS WER 1.26 (en).
- **GLM-4-Voice (9B, open)**: single-codebook 12.5 Hz supervised tokenizer (VQ on Whisper encoder, 175 bps) + **CosyVoice-based flow-matching decoder** (zai-org/glm-4-voice-decoder, streaming from 10 tokens); 1T speech-text token pretrain.
- **Kimi-Audio (7B, Apr 2025, MIT weights)**: 12.5 Hz semantic tokens (+Whisper continuous features in), **chunk-wise flow-matching detokenizer + BigVGAN** with look-ahead; **13M h** pretrain; LM init from Qwen2.5-7B.
- **Step-Audio 2 / 2-mini (Jul–Aug 2025, mini is Apache-2.0)**: CosyVoice 2 tokenizer (interleaved with text), flow-matching detokenizer → HiFi-GAN.
- **LFM2-Audio-1.5B / LFM2.5-Audio-1.5B (Oct 2025 / late 2025)**: closest published sibling to your design — 1.2B LFM2 backbone, FastConformer encoder in, **RQ-transformer over 8 Mimi codebooks** out, interleaved text/audio, <100 ms first audio. Competent but not naturalness-leading — evidence a ~1.5B Mimi-8 system sounds "fine," not "human."
- **MiMo-Audio (Xiaomi 7B, Sep 2025)**: own 25 Hz 8-layer RVQ, 4-frame patchification (LM at 6.25 Hz) + causal patch decoder; **>100M hours**; few-shot voice/emotion abilities *emerge only at that scale* — out of reach for a small team.
- **Kyutai TTS 1.6B / Unmute (Jul 2025, open)**: delayed-streams TTS, 1B backbone + 600M depth transformer, **Mimi with 16 codebooks**, 220 ms; Unmute = STT→LLM→TTS cascade, chosen by Kyutai themselves over Moshi-style end-to-end for quality.
- **2026 renderers/TTS**: **Mistral Voxtral TTS 4B (Mar 2026, CC-BY-NC-4.0, mistralai/Voxtral-4B-TTS-2603)**: 3.4B AR semantic-token decoder + **390M flow-matching acoustic transformer** + 300M causal codec at 12.5 Hz; blind tests 62.8% preferred over ElevenLabs Flash v2.5, parity with ElevenLabs v3. **Higgs Audio v2 3B (Boson, Aug 2025, ~Apache-2.0/open)**: 10M h, beats gpt-4o-mini-tts on EmergentTTS-Eval Emotions (75.7%); strong open choice (with Chatterbox-0.5B MIT) to replace Kokoro for synthesizing expressive assistant-side dialogue data.

## Scale-vs-naturalness reality
Moshi 7M h (flat voice), CSM 1M h (near-human without context), Kimi 13M h, MiMo 100M+ h, Higgs 10M h. Nobody has demonstrated human-like naturalness from an LM that must generate *acoustic* detail itself on <~1M h. Systems that offload acoustics to a pretrained flow-matching renderer (GLM-4-Voice, Kimi, Step-Audio) achieve high perceived quality with the LM seeing far fewer speech hours, because the renderer encapsulates the megahours.

## Ranked upgrade paths
1. **(ii) Semantic-token LM + pretrained flow-matching token2wav** — best quality per training hour at ≤1B. Exact renderers: zai-org/glm-4-voice-decoder (streaming, 12.5 Hz tokens, starts after 10 tokens ≈ 0.8 s) or Kimi-Audio's MIT chunk-streaming flow-matching+BigVGAN detokenizer; Apache alternative: Step-Audio-2-mini's CosyVoice2-token detokenizer (25 Hz).
2. **(i) 32-codebook Mimi + your existing depth transformer + 1/16 amortization** — cheapest change (same frozen codec, decode 32 quantizers ≈ 4.4 kbps), proven by CSM-1B, keeps 80 ms streaming and full-duplex; but CSM needed ~1M h, and LFM2-Audio shows Mimi-8-class output at 1.5B is mediocre.
3. **(iii) Qwen3-Omni-style MTP + causal-ConvNet code2wav** — best latency+quality but requires training your own codec/renderer and Qwen-scale data; adopt later, not now.

## KEY FACTS
- CSM uses Mimi split-RVQ at 12.5 Hz with 1 semantic + 31 acoustic codebooks (32 total); audio decoder trained on a random 1/16 subset of frames (zeroth codebook every frame) with no perceived quality loss; trained ~1M hours predominantly English, 5 epochs, 2048-token (~2 min) context; sizes 1B+100M, 3B+250M, 8B+300M; Apache-2.0 [https://www.sesame.com/research/crossing_the_uncanny_valley_of_voice]
- CSM CMOS study on Expresso: without conversational context listeners had no clear preference between CSM output and real human speech; with context humans still preferred; sesame/csm-1b weights on HF under Apache-2.0 [https://huggingface.co/sesame/csm-1b]
- The released kyutai/mimi checkpoint has num_quantizers=32 by default (codebook_size 2048, 12.5 Hz); encode/decode accept a num_quantizers argument, so 8 vs 16 vs 32 codebooks is a runtime choice on the same frozen codec (32 ≈ 4.4 kbps) [https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/model_doc/mimi]
- Moshi: 7B Helium temporal transformer + small depth transformer over Mimi (8 codebooks, 1.1 kbps); 160 ms theoretical / 200 ms practical latency; ~7M hours audio pretraining plus 20k hours synthetic dialogue; first real-time full-duplex spoken LLM [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2]
- Qwen2.5-Omni Talker token2wav = sliding-window DiT flow-matching (receptive field 4 blocks: 2 lookback, 1 lookahead) predicting mel, then modified BigVGAN vocoder; Thinker-Talker trained end-to-end; Apache-2.0 [https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/model_doc/qwen2_5_omni]
- Qwen3-Omni (Sep 2025): Talker is a 3B-A0.3B MoE that autoregressively predicts multi-codebook codecs via an MTP module; replaces block-wise DiT with a lightweight causal ConvNet Code2Wav for frame-by-frame streaming; 12.5 Hz codes; 234 ms end-to-end first-packet audio latency; 30B-A3B Instruct/Thinking released Apache-2.0 [https://arxiv.org/html/2509.17765v1]
- Qwen3.5-Omni (arXiv Apr 17, 2026) keeps Thinker-Talker + MTP + causal ConvNav code2wav, adds ARIA adaptive rate interleave alignment; 235-435 ms audio first-packet; SEED-TTS WER 0.99 zh / 1.26 en; API-only, no open weights [https://arxiv.org/html/2604.15804v1]
- GLM-4-Voice: single-codebook supervised speech tokenizer (VQ added to Whisper encoder) at 12.5 tokens/s; GLM-4-Voice-Decoder is a streaming flow-matching decoder retrained from CosyVoice that can start synthesis after only 10 speech tokens; 9B LLM pretrained on 1T speech-text tokens; code Apache-2.0, weights under GLM-4 model license, decoder at zai-org/glm-4-voice-decoder [https://github.com/zai-org/GLM-4-Voice/blob/main/README_en.md]
- Kimi-Audio (Apr 2025): hybrid input (Whisper continuous + 12.5 Hz discrete semantic tokens), LLM initialized from Qwen2.5-7B with dual text/audio heads, chunk-wise streaming flow-matching detokenizer + BigVGAN with look-ahead; pretrained on 13M hours; model weights MIT-licensed [https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18425]
- Step-Audio 2 uses the CosyVoice 2 tokenizer with text/audio tokens interleaved at a fixed ratio; detokenizer is flow-matching to mel + HiFi-GAN vocoder; Step-Audio-2-mini and mini-Base released under Apache-2.0 [https://huggingface.co/stepfun-ai/Step-Audio-2-mini]
- LFM2-Audio-1.5B (Liquid AI, Oct 1, 2025): 1.2B LFM2 backbone + ~115M FastConformer audio encoder in, RQ-transformer predicting 8-codebook Mimi tokens out, interleaved text/audio generation, <100 ms to first audio; successor LFM2.5-Audio-1.5B on HF [https://huggingface.co/LiquidAI/LFM2-Audio-1.5B]
- MiMo-Audio (Xiaomi, 7B): 25 Hz 8-layer RVQ tokenizer, 4-timestep patchification so LM runs at 6.25 Hz with causal patch decoder and delayed per-codebook generation; few-shot speech abilities (voice conversion, emotion transfer) emerge only past ~100M hours / trillions of tokens of pretraining [https://arxiv.org/html/2512.23808v1]
- Kyutai TTS (kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr): delayed streams modeling, 1B backbone + 600M depth transformer, Mimi at 12.5 Hz with 16 codebooks, ~220 ms latency, WER 2.82% EN, speaker similarity 77%+; powers Unmute (open STT->LLM->TTS cascade) [https://huggingface.co/kyutai/tts-1.6b-en_fr]
- Mistral Voxtral TTS (Mar 23-26, 2026): 4B total = 3.4B transformer decoder (semantic tokens) + 390M flow-matching acoustic transformer + 300M causal codec at 12.5 Hz; 62.8% of blind listeners preferred it over ElevenLabs Flash v2.5, parity with ElevenLabs v3; 3-s voice cloning, 9 languages; open weights mistralai/Voxtral-4B-TTS-2603 under CC-BY-NC-4.0 [https://mistral.ai/news/voxtral-tts/]
- Higgs Audio v2 (Boson AI, 3B, open on HF): pretrained on 10M+ hours; EmergentTTS-Eval win rates 75.7% (Emotions) and 55.7% (Questions) over gpt-4o-mini-tts; zero-shot multi-speaker dialogue generation — candidate to replace Kokoro for assistant-side synthetic dialogue [https://huggingface.co/bosonai/higgs-audio-v2-generation-3B-base]
- Resemble Chatterbox (0.5B, MIT): 63.75% of blind listeners preferred it over ElevenLabs in company study — another open option for expressive synthetic training speech [https://www.siliconflow.com/articles/en/best-open-source-text-to-speech-models]
- Sesame Maya/Miles demo (Feb 27, 2025) went viral; The Verge called it 'the first voice assistant I've ever wanted to talk to more than once'; Mar 2025 blind tests found participants initially could not distinguish CSM from humans in short conversations [https://research.contrary.com/company/sesame-ai]
- Sesame attributes the naturalness breakthrough to conditioning generation on full conversation history (context-aware prosody and timing) rather than isolated-utterance TTS; WER and speaker-similarity metrics are saturated/near-human across modern systems [https://www.sesame.com/research/crossing_the_uncanny_valley_of_voice]
- Data-hours ladder observed for naturalness: Moshi ~7M h (flat prosody), CSM ~1M h (near-human w/o context), Higgs Audio v2 10M h, Kimi-Audio 13M h, MiMo-Audio >100M h for emergent expressivity — no public system achieves human-like acoustic generation from an LM trained on much less than ~1M h unless acoustics are delegated to a pretrained flow-matching renderer [https://arxiv.org/html/2512.23808v1]

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt path (ii) as the primary upgrade: keep the from-scratch 12.5 Hz frame-grid backbone but have it emit a single low-rate token stream consumable by a frozen, pretrained flow-matching token2wav renderer, which supplies the megahours of acoustic naturalness we cannot train ourselves. Concretely: switch the audio output stream to the GLM-4-Voice 12.5 Hz single-codebook semantic tokens and render with zai-org/glm-4-voice-decoder (streaming from 10 tokens), or — for MIT/Apache license cleanliness — Kimi-Audio's chunk-streaming flow-matching+BigVGAN detokenizer or Step-Audio-2-mini's CosyVoice2-token detokenizer (Apache-2.0, 25 Hz). This collapses 8 codebooks×2048 to one stream, is easier for a ≤1B backbone, and matches what GLM-4-Voice, Kimi-Audio, Step-Audio 2, and Qwen2.5-Omni all converged on. Cost: ~0.5–1 s added first-audio latency vs codec-frame streaming; single-speaker timbre fixed by renderer prompt (fine for an assistant voice).

Keep path (i) as a low-cost parallel ablation since it is nearly free with existing code: decode 32 Mimi quantizers from the same frozen kyutai/mimi checkpoint (num_quantizers=32, ≈4.4 kbps), extend the existing CSM-style depth transformer to 32 levels with the 1/16-frame compute-amortization trick. Expect CSM-level quality only with ≥100k–1M hours; LFM2-Audio shows Mimi-8 at 1.5B sounds mediocre. Defer path (iii) (Qwen3-Omni MTP + causal ConvNet code2wav) — superior latency/quality but requires training a codec/renderer. Separately, replace Kokoro with Higgs Audio v2 (+Chatterbox) for expressive assistant-side dialogue synthesis; avoid Voxtral TTS (CC-BY-NC).
