# Training & inference techniques for naturalness in discrete-token speech LLMs (2024–2026)

## 1. Preference optimization (biggest proven post-SFT win)
- **SpeechAlign** (arXiv 2404.05600, Apr 2024): iterative DPO treating golden codec tokens as preferred vs model generations; improves WER and speaker-sim, works at small scale. Caveat: **Koel-TTS** (arXiv 2502.05236, NVIDIA, Feb 2025) found ground-truth-as-preferred is trivially separable and "leads to model degeneration" — prefer pairs drawn from the model's **own** samples, ranked by automatic metrics.
- **Koel-TTS recipe** (380M–1.1B, directly at your scale): sample N candidates, rank by CER (ASR) + speaker-sim (Titanet) via Pareto ranking, DPO/RPO. DPO alone: CER 2.56→0.62, SSIM 0.601→0.645; DPO+CFG: CER 0.58, SSIM 0.678; naturalness MOS 4.058 vs ground truth 3.937.
- **Seed-TTS** (2406.02430): REINFORCE with WER + WavLM-large-SV SIM rewards; hard-set WER 7.585→6.423, CMOS +0.14; SER-accuracy reward lifted angry-emotion accuracy 0.46→0.91. Warning: pure WER reward yields slow, over-articulated speech that *hurts* naturalness.
- **MPO** (2509.00685, Sep 2025): multidimensional preference sets (CER/Paraformer, WavLM-large SIM, log-F0 RMSE prosody), DPO + CE regularization, ~100 h preference data, lr 1e-6, **single A6000**: CER 4.72→3.90, SIM 0.548→0.577, ABX win 52.3% (ties 22.4%). Prosody as an explicit dimension is what moves perceived naturalness.
- **FPO** (2502.02950): token-level DPO computing loss only on error segments (mispronunciation, abnormal silence, repetition); CosyVoice CER 3.63→1.83, bad-case rate 21%→9%, NMOS +0.19, 3–4× less preference data.
- **RIO** (2407.02243): reward-model-free "reverse inference" self-preference for robustness. 2025-26 trend: GRPO for TTS (2509.18798, GLM-TTS 2512.14291, FlowTTS-GRPO).
- **Reward hacking is real**: RRPO (2512.04552, Dec 2025) showed policies generate mouth clicks/plosives that fool the reward model — naturalness MOS fell to 3.61 (below SFT 3.72); RM regularization (label smoothing, mixup, adversarial) recovered 3.81. Use multi-metric rewards + KL anchor; treat UTMOS-only rewards with suspicion.

## 2. Style/prosody conditioning
- **Orpheus-TTS** (github.com/canopyai/Orpheus-TTS, Llama-3B, Apache-2.0): paralinguistic special tokens `<laugh> <chuckle> <sigh> <cough> <sniffle> <groan> <yawn> <gasp>` in the text stream — trivially compatible with your frame-grid LM; needs tagged training data.
- **Parler-TTS** (Lyth & King, arXiv 2402.01912): natural-language style captions (gender, pitch, rate, monotony, reverb → LLM-written captions) via frozen Flan-T5; **CapSpeech** (2506.02863, Jun 2025) supplies a large style-captioned corpus/benchmark.
- **CSM/Sesame** (sesame.com blog, Mar 2025): multi-turn text+audio context conditioning gives contextual prosody; their CMOS-with-context evaluations show humans notice context-appropriateness.
- **Fixed-voice polish**: Moshi (2410.00037) got its consistent, natural voice by final-stage instruction tuning on synthetic dialogues rendered by their TTS conditioned on **one actor recorded in 70+ speaking styles** — voice locked "almost surely" without inference-time control. This final-stage single-voice finetune is the standard Moshi/CSM move and fits your half-duplex assistant.

## 3. Codebook loss weighting / masking
- Moshi weights **semantic codebook loss ×100** vs 1 for acoustic; text tokens masked 30% during pretraining; **acoustic delay of 1–2 frames "greatly improves" generation quality** (you already have delay machinery).
- CSM **compute amortization**: depth decoder trained on a random **1/16 of frames** (cb0 every frame) with "no perceivable difference" in decoder loss — large memory/throughput win for your CSM-mode.

## 4. Inference-time
- **CFG for discrete audio LMs (Koel-TTS)**: drop text+audio conditioning 10% during training; at decode, logits ℓ = γ·ℓ_cond + (1−γ)·ℓ_uncond, best γ=2.5. CER 2.56→0.69, SSIM 0.601→0.653 — the single largest inference-time gain; cost = 2× decode FLOPs (batchable, doubles effective batch). Selective-CFG (2509.19668) reduces the overhead.
- **Repetition-aware sampling (VALL-E 2**, 2406.05370): if token repetition ratio in a sliding window exceeds threshold, fall back from nucleus to random sampling — eliminates infinite loops/stuck silence; contributed to "human parity" robustness claims.
- Per-stream sampling: standard practice is lower temperature for text than audio and moderate top-k on audio (MusicGen top-k 250; CSM repo temp≈0.9/top-k 50); tune cb0 temperature separately from acoustic codebooks — cb0 errors are semantic, acoustic-cb diversity is mostly harmless.

## 5. Post-generation enhancement
- **Resemble-Enhance** (github.com/resemble-ai/resemble-enhance, HF ResembleAI/resemble-enhance): UNet denoiser + latent-CFM enhancer (IRMAE), outputs 44.1 kHz; known to help TTS/codec output but can add flow-matching artifacts; quality ceiling = its 44.1 kHz training data.
- **Miipher-2** (2505.04457, Google, May 2025): USM-conditioned restoration + WaveFit, RTF 0.0078, no text/speaker conditioning; weights not open, but **Open-Miipher-2** (github.com/yukara-ikemiya/Open-Miipher-2) is a PyTorch reimplementation. Positioned mainly as *training-data cleaning* — arguably the higher-leverage use (clean/restore your expressive corpora before tokenization).
- Bandwidth extension 24→48 kHz: **AP-BWE** (2401.06387; 292× realtime on RTX 4090) and **FlashSR** (one-step distilled diffusion) are the current cheap options; gains are modest on already-clean 24 kHz Mimi output and can add artifacts — A/B before shipping.

## 6. Evaluation battery — with a big caveat
**TTSDS2** (2506.19441, SSW 2025): of 16 metrics, **only TTSDS2 keeps Spearman >0.5 with human MOS in every domain** (clean 0.75, noisy 0.59, wild 0.75, kids 0.61). **UTMOS avg ≈0.15 and UTMOSv2 ≈0.20 on modern generated speech; WER correlates negatively (≈−0.23); SQuIM-MOS ≈0.57; speaker-sim (X-vector/RawNet3) ≈0.60.** So: use WER (whisper-large-v3), speaker-sim (WavLM/ECAPA), DistillMOS (2502.05356, microsoft/Distill-MOS — compact XLS-R-SQA distillate, 100k+ rated clips) as cheap regression tests and RL rewards, but gate releases on TTSDS2 + small human CMOS, especially for conversational output where UTMOS-class predictors collapse.

## KEY FACTS
- Koel-TTS (NVIDIA, 380M–1.1B) applies classifier-free guidance to discrete-token TTS by dropping text+audio conditioning with 10% probability in training and interpolating logits with gamma=2.5 at inference; CER 2.56%→0.69%, speaker-sim 0.601→0.653, at 2x decode compute [https://arxiv.org/html/2502.05236v2]
- Koel-TTS preference alignment (DPO/RPO over model's own samples Pareto-ranked by Parakeet-ASR CER + Titanet speaker-sim) gives CER 2.56→0.62 and SSIM 0.601→0.645; combined with CFG reaches CER 0.58 / SSIM 0.678 and naturalness MOS 4.058 vs ground-truth 3.937 [https://arxiv.org/html/2502.05236v2]
- Koel-TTS found using ground-truth audio as the 'preferred' sample in DPO fails — trivially separable from generations and 'leads to model degeneration' — contradicting SpeechAlign's golden-token approach [https://arxiv.org/html/2502.05236v2]
- SpeechAlign (Apr 2024) pioneered iterative DPO on codec LMs (golden tokens preferred vs generated), improving WER and speaker similarity and working for small models [https://arxiv.org/html/2404.05600v1]
- Seed-TTS RL (REINFORCE, rewards = WER + WavLM-large-SV similarity) cut hard-set WER 7.585→6.423 and CMOS +0.14; SER-accuracy reward raised angry-emotion accuracy 0.46→0.91; but WER-reward causes slow over-articulated speech that sacrifices naturalness [https://arxiv.org/html/2406.02430v1]
- MPO (Sep 2025) does DPO with multidimensional preference sets (CER via Paraformer, WavLM-large speaker-sim, log-F0-RMSE prosody) on ~100h preference data on a single A6000 (lr 1e-6, DPO+CE loss): CER 4.72→3.90, SIM 0.548→0.577, ABX win 52.3% vs baseline [https://arxiv.org/html/2509.00685v1]
- FPO (Feb 2025) computes preference loss only on error token segments (abnormal silence, repetition, mispronunciation); on CosyVoice: CER 3.63→1.83, bad-case ratio 21%→9%, NMOS +0.19, with 3–4x fewer training samples than utterance-level DPO [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2502.02950]
- RRPO (Dec 2025) documented reward hacking in TTS RL: policy generated mouth clicks/plosives fooling the reward model, dropping naturalness MOS to 3.61 (below SFT 3.72); regularizing the RM (label smoothing, energy-adaptive mixup, adversarial training) recovered naturalness MOS 3.81 [https://arxiv.org/html/2512.04552v1]
- Moshi weights the semantic codebook (cb0) loss at alpha=100 vs 1 for acoustic codebooks, masks text tokens 30% during pretraining, and reports an acoustic delay of 1–2 frames between semantic and acoustic tokens 'greatly improves the quality of the generation' [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2]
- Moshi's consistent final voice came from instruction tuning on synthetic dialogues rendered by their TTS conditioned on a single actor who recorded monologues in 70+ speaking styles, locking the voice 'almost surely' without inference-time control [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.00037v2]
- Sesame CSM trains its depth/audio decoder on a random 1/16 subset of frames (cb0 on every frame) — 'compute amortization' — with no perceivable difference in audio decoder loss [https://www.sesame.com/blog/crossing-the-uncanny-valley-of-voice]
- Orpheus-TTS (Llama-3B backbone, Apache-2.0) implements paralinguistics as special text tokens: <laugh>, <chuckle>, <sigh>, <cough>, <sniffle>, <groan>, <yawn>, <gasp> [https://github.com/canopyai/Orpheus-TTS]
- Parler-TTS conditions on natural-language style captions (gender, pitch, speaking rate, monotony, reverberation auto-annotated then LLM-captioned) via a frozen Flan-T5 encoder; CapSpeech (Jun 2025) provides a large style-captioned TTS dataset/benchmark [https://arxiv.org/html/2506.02863v2]
- VALL-E 2's repetition-aware sampling replaces nucleus samples with random samples when the token repetition ratio in a sliding window exceeds a threshold, eliminating infinite-loop/silence failure modes and contributing to claimed human parity on LibriSpeech/VCTK [https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.05370]
- TTSDS2 (SSW 2025): across clean/noisy/wild/kids domains, UTMOS averages Spearman ~0.15 and UTMOSv2 ~0.20 with human ratings of modern TTS, WER correlates negatively (~-0.23), SQuIM-MOS ~0.57, speaker-sim ~0.60; TTSDS2 is the only metric of 16 above 0.5 in every domain (avg 0.67) [https://arxiv.org/html/2506.19441v1]
- Distill-MOS (Microsoft, ICASSP 2025, arXiv 2502.05356) is a compact speech-quality model distilled from an XLS-R-based teacher retrained on 100k+ MOS-labeled clips, two orders of magnitude smaller; weights at github.com/microsoft/Distill-MOS [https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05356]
- Miipher-2 (Google, May 2025) restores speech using frozen USM features + WaveFit vocoder, no text/speaker conditioning, RTF 0.0078 (million hours in ~3 days on 100 accelerators); weights closed but Open-Miipher-2 is a PyTorch reimplementation [https://arxiv.org/html/2505.04457v4]
- Resemble-Enhance (open source, HF ResembleAI/resemble-enhance) = UNet denoiser + latent conditional-flow-matching enhancer (IRMAE) trained on 44.1 kHz speech; effective on TTS/codec output but quality-capped by its training data and can introduce CFM artifacts [https://github.com/resemble-ai/resemble-enhance]
- AP-BWE (GAN, parallel amplitude+phase prediction) does 24→48 kHz bandwidth extension at 292x realtime on an RTX 4090 (18x realtime on CPU); FlashSR offers one-step distilled-diffusion 48 kHz audio super-resolution [https://arxiv.org/html/2401.06387v2]
- 2025–26 trend: online GRPO applied to LLM-based TTS (GRPO-for-TTS arXiv 2509.18798, GLM-TTS technical report arXiv 2512.14291, FlowTTS-GRPO arXiv 2606.23190) with multi-objective rewards (WER, SIM, emotion), extending the DPO recipes with on-policy sampling [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18798]

## RECOMMENDATION
Adopt, in order of measured effect per GPU-hour: (1) **CFG-ready training now**: drop text/audio conditioning 10% during pretraining so you get Koel-TTS-style guidance for free; decode with gamma≈2–2.5. Largest single verified gain (CER 2.56→0.69, SSIM +0.05) for 2x decode FLOPs — batchable, still streamable. (2) **Post-SFT DPO at MPO/Koel scale**: sample 4–8 candidates per prompt on ~100 h of dialogue text, rank with whisper-large-v3 WER + WavLM/ECAPA speaker-sim + F0-RMSE prosody + DistillMOS (never MOS-predictor alone — RRPO-documented reward hacking), train DPO+CE at lr 1e-6. Days on your 8xGPU node; expect CER down 20–50%, ABX naturalness wins ~2:1. Add FPO-style error-segment masking if data-limited. (3) **Keep/confirm Moshi settings**: cb0 loss x100, acoustic delay 1–2, 30% text masking; adopt CSM 1/16 depth-decoder amortization for throughput. (4) **Paralinguistic tags** (Orpheus-style special tokens) plus optional style captions in the text stream — near-zero architecture cost, requires tagged data (CapSpeech). (5) **Final single-voice polish** on one expressive speaker across many styles (Moshi recipe) — this, not speaker embeddings, is how Moshi/CSM got their voices. (6) **Decode hygiene**: repetition-aware sampling (VALL-E 2), separate cb0 vs acoustic temperatures. (7) Post-enhancement (resemble-enhance / AP-BWE 24→48 kHz) only after A/B — modest, artifact-prone. Evaluate with WER + speaker-sim + DistillMOS as CI regression metrics, but gate on TTSDS2 + small human CMOS: UTMOS(v2) correlates ~0.15–0.20 with humans on modern generated conversational speech.
